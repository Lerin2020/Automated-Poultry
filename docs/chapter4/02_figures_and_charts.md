## B. Egg counting & sensors (section 4.3.2 / 4.4.1)

10. **Sensor detection-distance reliability** — detection success rate (%) vs.
    egg distance from the E18-D80NK sensor (bar or line). Justifies sensor
    mounting position.
11. **Debounce justification histogram** — distribution of time gaps between
    consecutive egg detections; show the 200 ms debounce window as a shaded
    region proving real eggs are never that close together.
12. **Error-source breakdown** — count of miscounts by cause across all trials:
    double-count, missed egg, vibration false trigger (bar or pie).
13. **Cumulative egg yield** — running total of eggs over the data-collection
    period, Layer 1 vs. Layer 2 (stacked area or two lines).
14. **Layer comparison** — eggs per day per layer (stacked bar): shows the
    per-layer counting feature doing useful work.
15. **Belt speed vs. counting accuracy** (if you varied it) — accuracy % at
    different belt speeds (line). Identifies the operating limit.

## C. Feeding system (4.3.1)

16. **Feed distribution uniformity** — mass of feed (g) collected in each
    segment of the trough after one gantry pass (bar). The key "does the
    gantry actually distribute evenly?" evidence.
17. **Feed dispensed per cycle repeatability** — grams dispensed across 10
    cycles with mean line (bar/line). Shows consistent ration delivery.
18. **Gantry return-home position error** — distance from true home after each
    cycle (scatter or bar). Honest evidence on the open-loop limitation.

## D. Environment & timekeeping (4.3.4 / 4.3.5)

19. **DHT22 calibration scatter** — DHT22 reading vs. reference thermometer
    with y = x line and regression; report R². Same optionally for humidity.
20. **Multi-day environmental log** — temperature (and humidity) over 3–7 days
    (line), annotated with any alert events.
21. **Schedule firing accuracy** — deviation (seconds) between scheduled hour
    and actual cycle start across all scheduled runs (scatter or bar).
22. **RTC drift** — DS3231 time offset vs. phone clock measured daily over a
    week (line). Typically tiny — an easy "excellent result".
23. **Alert latency** — time from condition (≥ 40 °C / threshold reached) to
    alert appearing on dashboard (bar across trials).

## E. Networking & dashboard (4.4.5 / 4.4.3)

24. **MQTT round-trip latency distribution** — box plot of publish→echo times
    over ~50 samples. Stronger than a single mean.
25. **Dashboard load time by device** — first-load time on phone / laptop /
    tablet (bar). Shows the gzipped-SPIFFS serving works on anything.
26. **WebSocket reconnection time** — time for dashboard to auto-reconnect
    after broker restart / WiFi blip, across trials (bar).
27. **WiFi range test** — RSSI and command success rate vs. distance from the
    cage (dual-axis line). Practical deployment guidance.
28. **System availability** — uptime % over the test period, with any resets
    annotated (single bar or timeline).

## F. Power, cost & comparison (4.4.6 / 4.5)

29. **Power consumption per subsystem** — measured current/power for ESP32
    idle, gantry+auger running, belts running, conveyor running (bar).
30. **Estimated daily energy use** — Wh/day given the twice-daily schedules
    (stacked bar per subsystem); extend to monthly cost at local tariff.
31. **Cost comparison** — your BOM total vs. 2–3 commercial poultry automation
    products (bar, log scale if gap is large). A favourite with examiners.
32. **Feature comparison radar/matrix** — your system vs. commercial/related
    works on: cost, offline operation, egg counting, scheduling, monitoring.
    (A ✓/✗ table also works — see 03_tables.md.)

---
**Chart-choice rules of thumb**
- Comparing categories → bar; trends over time → line; distributions → histogram
  or box plot; calibration/agreement → scatter with y = x; parts of a whole →
  stacked bar (avoid pies unless 2–3 slices).
- Always: axis labels with units, caption below figure, reference in the text.
